Problem Solving: Addition

Objectives:

• Develop problem-solving skills using addition.
• Learn strategies for solving addition problems.

Examples:

1. Solve the following problems:
Jane has 24 apples, and she buys 30 more. How many apples does she have now?: 54
Tom has 45 marbles, and he finds 25 more. How many marbles does he have now?: 70
A bakery sold 60 cakes in the morning and 20 more in the afternoon. How many cakes were sold in total?: 80
Sara has 33 books, and she receives 44 more as gifts. How many books does she have now?: 77
Mike saved 52 dollars and earned 38 more. How much money does he have now?: 90
